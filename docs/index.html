Significant Pair Survival Under Confidence Sensitivity

Axis Meaning, Formula, and Interpretation

X-axis: Confidence threshold (%), from 80 to 99.

Y-axis: Estimated number of pairwise gaps that remain statistically significant.

Formula: \(\hat{N}_{std}(c) \approx N_{std,95}(z_{95}/z_c)^{1.05}\), \(\hat{N}_{hier}(c) \approx N_{hier,95}(z_{95}/z_c)^{1.12}\).

Inference: As confidence strictness rises, both curves fall; the hierarchical curve falls faster, revealing fragility hidden under standard BTL assumptions.

Variance Decomposition Across Models

Axis Meaning, Formula, and Interpretation

X-axis: Model names (filtered by Top-N control).

Y-axis: Either absolute variance component values or percent share.

Formula: \(V_{total} \approx V_{sampling}+V_{prompt}+V_{distance}+V_{interaction}\).

Inference: Large interaction segments indicate structural uncertainty dominates simple sampling noise for many frontier models.

Top Flipped Winners (Fragility Hotspots)

Axis Meaning, Formula, and Interpretation

X-axis: Winner model under standard BTL among flipped pairs.

Y-axis: Count of flipped wins that disappear under hierarchical uncertainty.

Formula: count\((winner\_model\_under\_standard)\) over flipped pair set.

Inference: Taller bars identify where standard-significant claims are most vulnerable to robust uncertainty correction.

Win Probability Comparison

Axis Meaning, Formula, and Interpretation

X-axis: Selected Model A and Model B.

Y-axis: Estimated matchup win probability (%).

Formula: \(p_A=\sigma((S_A-S_B)\cdot 3.6-penalty)\), \(p_B=1-p_A\).

Inference: Wider separation means stronger directional confidence in the matchup.

Robustness Under Noise

Axis Meaning, Formula, and Interpretation

X-axis: Selected Model A and Model B.

Y-axis: Robustness score (%), higher means less uncertainty-driven instability.

Formula: Robustness is normalized from model variance and interaction uncertainty: \((1-\tilde{V}_{total})\) and \((1-pct_{interaction})\).

Inference: Models with higher robustness sustain significance claims better under stricter uncertainty modeling.

Interaction Risk Profile

Axis Meaning, Formula, and Interpretation

X-axis: Selected Model A and Model B.

Y-axis: Interaction-risk score (%), proxied by interaction-related uncertainty share.

Formula: Risk \(\approx pct_{interaction}\).

Inference: Higher interaction risk means pairwise conclusions are more likely to flip under robust uncertainty treatment.

Story 01

The headline certainty shrinks under realistic noise

Standard leaderboard significance treats all votes as IID, but your hierarchical model reclassifies a major share of frontier gaps as statistically indistinguishable.

Story 02

Most uncertainty is structural, not sampling noise

Variance is dominated by interaction effects, so collecting more battles alone will not solve confidence inflation in close matchups.

Story 03

Fragility concentrates among top-tier model comparisons

Flipped pairs cluster around strongest models, which is where benchmark-based procurement and deployment choices are most sensitive.
